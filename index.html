PAC-MAN, is an arcade game developed by Namco and first released in Japan in May 1980.[2][3] It was created by Japanese video game designer Toru Iwatani. It was licensed for distribution in the United States by Midway Games and released in October 1980. The most visible minority were sports games that were mostly derivatives of Pong. Pac-Man succeeded by creating a new genre.

Pac-Man is...

  • often credited with being a landmark in video game history and is among the most famous arcade games of all time
  • also one of the highest-grossing video games of all time,[14] having generated more than $2.5 billion in quarters by the 1990s
  • one of the longest running video game franchises from the golden age of video arcade games
  • part of the collection of the Smithsonian Institution in Washington, D.C.[21] and New York's Museum of Modern Art
